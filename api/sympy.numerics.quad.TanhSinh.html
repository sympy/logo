Class s.n.q.TanhSinh(Quadrature):

Part of sympy.numerics.quad

The tanh-sinh quadrature rule (also known as the doubly-exponential quadrature rule) is well suited for integrals with singularities and/or extremely high precision levels (tens or hundreds of digits).

The level m is a small integer between, say, 5 for low precision levels and 10 for extremely high precision (it must be set higher if the integrand is ill-behaved).

This implementation of the tanh-sinh algorithm is based on the description given in Borwein, Bailey & Girgensohn, "Experimentation in Mathematics - Computational Paths to Discovery", A K Peters, 2003, pages 312-313. It also described in various documents available online.
